SAWM

June 2024

Figma.

An app prototype I worked on in a group of four as part of my Human-Computer Interaction course. We set out to create a social media space for Muslim international students in non-Muslim-majority countries, helping them stay connected with friends and family back home during Ramadan. Through multiple rounds of user testing and iteration, we developed this high-fidelity prototype of what the app could look like.

Quick! Madlibs

December 2022

p5.js + ml5.js

A game I built with a partner using a machine learning library that recognizes hand positions and another that classifies images. The webcam records how the player draws in the air using their finger, and the AI attempts to classify the drawing. The result is then used to fill in the blanks of a randomly generated Mad Libs story that is given to the player at the end, creating unexpected stories each time.

The House

December 2022

Analog Board Game.

A fully analog, choose-your-own-adventure board game that I made with a group of four. I did a lot of the writing, making sure the branching paths were complex but still made sense no matter which way you approached the story, with plenty of hidden Easter eggs. It's the kind of game where you might not catch everything the first time, but the more you keep playing the more you'll find.
